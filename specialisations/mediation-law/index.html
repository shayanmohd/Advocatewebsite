Mediation & Dispute Resolution

Facilitating out-of-court resolutions for family and business disputes through structured mediation — preserving relationships, reputations and time.

  1. Home
  2. Practice
  3. Mediation & Dispute Resolution

An ADR-led practice for parties who would rather negotiate a workable outcome than be handed an imperfect order years later.

The Mediation Act, 2023 codifies a long-overdue framework for settled mediations in India — granting them the binding force of a decree of court when properly registered. The chamber drafts with the Act's requirements in view to ensure the settlement, once reached, is enforceable in fact and not merely in spirit.

The chamber's approach is to begin with a clear written opinion on prospects and risk, agree the strategy in writing with the client, and then execute — with regular briefings and full visibility throughout. Fees are quoted transparently after the initial consultation, with phased and milestone-based billing available where appropriate.

Services Offered

What the chamber handles in this area.

01

Court-Annexed Mediation

Conduct and representation in mediation proceedings referred under section 89 of the CPC and section 442 of the BNSS — including the Delhi Mediation Centre.

02

Pre-Litigation Mediation

Compulsory pre-litigation mediation under section 12A of the Commercial Courts Act, 2015 — drafting demand notices, conducting joint sessions and recording settlements.

03

Family Mediation

Sensitive facilitation of divorce, custody, maintenance and family-property disputes — with particular care for child welfare and confidentiality.

04

Mediation Settlement Agreements

Drafting enforceable settlement agreements under the Mediation Act, 2023 — registered, witnessed and stamped to withstand future challenge.

05

Commercial Mediation

Resolution of contractual, joint-venture and shareholder disputes — with mediated terms that survive the relationship.

06

Conciliation under the Arbitration Act

Conciliation proceedings under Part III of the Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996, including the appointment of conciliators and the recording of settled terms.
