Biography

Counsel of conviction. Advocacy of distinction.

Advocate Mohd Yunus is a senior counsel based in New Delhi with a decade of practice across the Supreme Court of India, the Delhi High Court and the trial courts of Delhi — including Saket, Patiala House and Tis Hazari. The chamber represents corporations, public figures and private clients in matters that demand both technical mastery and steady courtcraft.

Born and raised in Okhla, Advocate Yunus carries forward a long family tradition of public service. His decision to read law was shaped by the constitutional guarantees that protect citizens against the asymmetries of power — a conviction that continues to anchor the chamber's preference for hard-won justice over easy settlement, and for honest counsel over comfortable advice.

The practice is anchored in two flagship areas. Mining law, where he advises operators, investors and landowners on leases, royalty disputes, environmental clearances and proceedings before the National Green Tribunal. And criminal defence, where he appears in matters of bail, anticipatory bail, trial advocacy, white-collar offences, PMLA proceedings and CBI / ED investigations — handled with the discretion sensitive briefs require.

While the chamber is rooted in Delhi, Advocate Yunus regularly appears before the District & Sessions Courts and the High Courts of Uttar Pradesh, Haryana and Punjab — particularly in mining, criminal and matrimonial matters that travel outside the capital. The chamber maintains long-standing local-counsel relationships in Lucknow, Allahabad, Chandigarh and Gurugram to ensure clients receive seamless representation wherever the brief takes them.

Beyond litigation, the chamber maintains a robust civil and commercial advisory practice — drafting and negotiating contracts, structuring estate plans, and acting as mediator in family and business disputes. A meaningful share of the chamber's calendar is dedicated to pro-bono representation for matters of genuine need.

A commitment to public welfare runs in the family. The Yunus family has historically donated land to Jamia Millia Islamia and to several other educational, religious and community institutions in and around Okhla — a legacy of philanthropy that continues to inform the chamber's ethic of service. Advocate Yunus is an active member of the Saket Court Bar Association and an esteemed member of the Indian Islamic Cultural Centre, New Delhi, where he engages with the wider community on questions of law, education and civic life.

Drawing on a wide professional network across India's institutional, political and bureaucratic corridors, Advocate Yunus is well placed to navigate complex regulatory mandates and high-profile briefs with the discretion they demand. The chamber's reputation rests on three habits — preparation that is exhaustive, advice that is honest, and representation that is personal.
